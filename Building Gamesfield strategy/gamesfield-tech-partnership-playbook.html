Partnerships
The Higgsfield Playbook
How Higgsfield got volume deals and deep integrations — and how Gamesfield replicates it.
How Higgsfield Did It
Higgsfield didn't build AI models. They aggregated them, got volume deals, and shared revenue with partners. Here's the exact playbook.
01
Built the product first, negotiated deals after. They launched with standard API pricing, grew volume, then approached providers with real usage data. No one gives volume deals to zero-revenue startups.
02
Featured providers prominently. Every Higgsfield video shows "Made with Kling" or "Powered by Flux." Partners get brand exposure to millions of creators — that's the value exchange that unlocks deals.
03
Became a distribution channel. AI model companies need users. Higgsfield brings them users at scale. At 15M users, they're a top-5 distribution partner for most of their providers.
04
Formalised as revenue-share partnerships. The mature form: Higgsfield takes 70% of each credit used for a model, pays the provider 30%. Provider gets volume + distribution, Higgsfield gets margin.
05
Used deals as a moat. Once a provider is integrated and on rev-share, they have no incentive to integrate a competitor. Gamesfield should sign these deals as early as possible.
